# Decision Gates and Quantitative Thresholds

Use these gates to decide progression from discovery -> fake-door -> pilot -> build.

## Budget Assumptions
- Define your total Week 1-2 ad/traffic budget before starting (suggested: $100-200 total).
- All cost thresholds below are relative to your declared budget.

## Intra-Week Kill Criteria (apply to every week)
- If a channel spends 30% of its allocated budget with zero qualified signups, pause that channel immediately.
- If total spend reaches 50% of weekly budget with fewer than 5 qualified signups across all channels, stop and reassess messaging/targeting before spending more.
- If landing page receives 100+ visits with <2% signup rate, the page/offer needs revision before more traffic.

---

## Gate 1: Signal Discovery (end of Week 1)

### Purpose
Determine whether any ICP/message combination shows early demand signal worth investing in.

### Must Meet
- At least 2 acquisition channels tested with tagged traffic.
- At least 150 targeted landing-page visits total.
- At least one ICP/message pair achieves >=5% visitor-to-signup conversion.
- At least 10 qualified signups total.
- Competitor research completed with positioning gaps identified.

### Decision
- Pass: invest Week 2 budget into the winning ICP/message pair(s).
- Partial: signal exists but weak — iterate message/page and retest with remaining budget.
- Fail: no conversion signal after full budget — reassess product concept or ICP before spending more.

---

## Gate 2: Demand Confirmation (end of Week 2)

### Purpose
Confirm demand signal is repeatable and economically viable, not a fluke from Week 1.

### Must Meet
- Best ICP/message pair sustains >=8% visitor-to-signup conversion at higher volume (300+ visits).
- At least 25 cumulative qualified signups.
- At least 20% of qualified signups perform a high-intent action (demo request, waitlist confirmation, "ready to test" response).
- Cost per qualified signup is within declared budget cap.
- At least 2 distinct channels show positive signal (not dependent on single source).

### Decision
- Pass: lock MVP scope and move to Week 3.
- Fail: test alternative ICP angle or value proposition before expanding scope.

---

## Gate 3: MVP Scope Quality (end of Week 3)

### Must Meet
- Single core flow documented: screenshot -> draft -> review -> status update.
- No more than 5 core features in v1 scope.
- Every v1 feature mapped to at least one observed demand/objection signal from Week 1-2.
- Clear non-goals list approved.

### Decision
- Pass: proceed to architecture/cost/privacy decisions.
- Fail: reduce scope until all features map to evidence.

---

## Gate 4: Feasibility and Economics (end of Week 4)

### Must Meet
- Base-case contribution margin positive at target starter price.
- AI extraction unit cost modeled for low/base/high scenarios with at least 2 model options compared.
- High-severity privacy risks have mitigation owner and plan.
- Platform decision made (iOS-first vs Android-first, RN vs native) with weighted score.

### Decision
- Pass: prep pilot.
- Fail: adjust pricing, scope, or technical approach.

---

## Gate 5: Pilot Readiness (end of Week 5)

### Must Meet
- 5-10 pilot users committed (recruited from qualified signups or community outreach).
- Success metrics instrumented: extraction accuracy, correction time, order completion progression.
- Support and escalation workflow documented.

### Decision
- Pass: run pilot.
- Fail: fill readiness gaps before launch.

---

## Gate 6: Pilot Outcome (end of Week 6)

### Must Meet
- Extraction field accuracy reaches >=80% for key fields (name, product, price, quantity).
- Median time-to-confirm draft is under 60 seconds.
- At least 40% pilot users remain active through week end.
- At least 2-3 users show explicit willingness to pay (deposit, pre-order, paid trial).

### Decision
- Go: begin focused build.
- Pivot: adjust ICP/workflow/pricing.
- Stop: if adoption and economics remain weak after iteration.
