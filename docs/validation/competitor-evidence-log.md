## Positioning Gaps Observed

1. No English-language tool focuses on screenshot-first order capture. All competitors assume API integration or manual inbox.
2. Existing options are complex for solo sellers with low process maturity.
3. Room for "capture first, structure later" positioning vs "set up your CRM first."
4. Main risk: users may see this as "another CRM" unless onboarding is radically simple. Never use "CRM" in seller-facing copy.
5. Pricing gravity: solo sellers pay $0–$15/mo for tools. Above $15/mo needs strong justification.
6. The real baseline may be camera roll + memory, not spreadsheets. Messaging must address this.
7. Screenshot-to-DM ordering is already a standard behavior instructed by sellers to buyers — confirming screenshot familiarity in the ICP.

## Open Validation Questions

1. Is screenshot upload a compelling behavior shortcut vs copying text?
2. Which extracted fields are truly required on day one?
3. Is "do not lose orders" stronger than "save admin time" as core message?
4. What monthly price feels "no-brainer" for solo sellers with <100 orders/month?
5. Do sellers already screenshot conversations? How often?
6. Is WhatsApp co-primary (or primary) with Instagram for order coordination in the target ICP?
7. Does the ICP self-identify with "handmade/custom" or "Instagram seller" as the stronger identity label?

## Behavioral Evidence Collected (Week 1)

- Public Instagram posts show sellers instructing buyers: "Screenshot the product and send via DM to confirm availability" — validates screenshot familiarity in ICP.
- DM Tracker's own copy confirms: "most people stop updating their spreadsheet within 1–2 weeks" — validates low-maturity baseline.
- Etsy sellers face similar fragmented data pain (orders in Etsy, materials in spreadsheets, no connection).
- Sellers at <$10k/mo revenue explicitly use manual free tools; above that, some graduate to Craftybase or Kommo.

## Confidence Assessment (Updated 2026-03-30)
- High that screenshot behavior already exists in ICP niche (behavioral evidence observed).
- High that no English-language competitor owns the screenshot-first angle.
- Medium on market signal (competitors exist but none own the screenshot angle).
- Low-to-medium on pricing assumptions (need behavioral data from landing page).
- High that manual substitutes (notes/sheets/camera roll) are the true baseline competitor.
- Low on WhatsApp as co-primary channel (unvalidated; add intake question to landing page).
